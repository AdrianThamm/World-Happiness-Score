WHICH COUNTRY IS THE HAPPIEST?

The World Happiness Report is published by the Sustainable Development Solutions Network that uses data from the Gallup World Poll and Lloydâ€™s Register Foundation. It is written by a group of independent experts and the opinions stated in the report do not necessarily reflect the views of any United Nations organisation, agency, or programme. Each variable measured reveals a populated-weighted average score on a scale of 0 to 10, which is then monitored through time and compared to other nations. These variables are levels of GDP, healthy life expectancy, generosity, social support, freedom to make life choices, and perceptions of corruption. Each country is also compared to a hypothetical country called Dystopia that has national averages for each of the six variables equal to the lowest in the world. Dystopia and residual error are being used as a regression benchmark.

Distribution of Happiness Score By Region In 2021

Most countries in the Sub-Saharan African region has happiness score between 3 to 5.4 due to the reason that most of them are underdeveloped countries that lack social supports. Whereas most of the countries in Western Europe have happiness score of 7 and above .

Changes In Rank of Top 10 Happiest Countries From 2005 to 2020

Finland has ranked the happiest country in the world five times in a row since 2016. Denmark was also ranked the happiest country in the world five times in a row from 2007 to 2011. Furthermore, Canada rose from eighth place in 2012 to first place in 2013.

Does Happier Countries Have Higher Health Life Expectancy?

R-squared value of 0.589977 shows that there is moderate positive linear association between healthy life expectancy and happiness score.

The Extend of Freedom Contributed to the Calculation of Happiness Score of Each Country From 2005 to 2020

Finland , the happiest country in the world has consistent and high degree of freedom contributing to its happiness score throughout the years. The extend of freedom contributing to its happiness score for Pakistan has also increases steadily every year. Whereas the extend of freedom to make life choices contributes to its happiness score for Afghanistan has started to decrease since 2009 and it reach as low as 0.374 in 2018.
